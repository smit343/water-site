Water Treatment Plant Operator

Location: Central Treatment Plant | Job Type: Full-time | Pay: $25 - $35/hr

Position Overview:

We are seeking a qualified and motivated Water Treatment Plant Operator to oversee and manage the daily operations of our modern water treatment facility. The ideal candidate will ensure that water treatment processes meet safety, environmental, and regulatory standards, while maintaining optimal plant performance.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Monitor, operate, and maintain water treatment equipment and systems.
  • Collect and analyze water samples to ensure quality standards are met.
  • Record operational data and prepare regular reports.
  • Perform routine maintenance and minor repairs on mechanical components.
  • Respond to alarms, troubleshoot issues, and take corrective action as needed.
  • Ensure compliance with EPA and local water quality regulations.

Qualifications:

  • High school diploma or GED (required).
  • Valid Class B or higher Water Treatment Operator Certification.
  • 2+ years of experience in water or wastewater plant operations preferred.
  • Strong mechanical and analytical skills.
  • Ability to work rotating shifts, weekends, and respond to emergencies.

Work Conditions & Benefits:

  • Full-time position with rotating shift schedules.
  • Competitive hourly wage: $25–$35/hr depending on experience.
  • Health, dental, vision insurance, and retirement plan.
  • PPE and training provided.

Customer Service Representative

Location: Downtown Office | Job Type: Full-time | Pay: $20 - $28/hr

Position Overview:

As a Customer Service Representative at WROS, you will serve as the frontline connection between the public and our wastewater utility services. You will handle inquiries, service requests, and account support, ensuring a high level of customer satisfaction while navigating utility policies and systems.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Answer customer phone calls, emails, and in-person inquiries regarding wastewater services, billing, and account management.
  • Assist with service requests, account creation/termination, and payment plans.
  • Document all customer interactions and maintain accurate service records.
  • Collaborate with field service teams and billing department to resolve escalated issues.
  • Educate customers about conservation efforts and service guidelines.

Qualifications:

  • High school diploma or GED (required); Associate’s degree preferred.
  • 1–2 years of experience in customer service, preferably in a utility or municipal environment.
  • Strong communication, problem-solving, and conflict resolution skills.
  • Ability to handle high call volumes and multitask effectively.
  • Familiarity with CRM systems or utility billing software is a plus.

Work Conditions & Benefits:

  • Full-time office-based position (Monday–Friday, 8am–5pm).
  • Pay range: $20–$28/hr based on experience and qualifications.
  • Health, dental, and vision insurance; retirement plan; paid leave.
  • Ongoing training and career advancement opportunities within WROS.
